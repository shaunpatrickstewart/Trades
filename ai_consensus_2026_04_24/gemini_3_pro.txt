### 5. Traps & Gotchas (Second-Order Effects)

**Trap A: The "Stop-Loss" Fiction on Polymarket**
Polymarket is a CLOB, not a continuous broker. If bad news breaks, the bid side of the book vaporizes instantly. You will not get filled at a 30% loss; the price will gap to $0.01. 
* **If wrong:** The bot assumes a 30% loss, sizes for a 73% WR, hits three 100% gap-down losses, and you take a catastrophic hit outside your Kelly model. 
* **Fix:** The bot must dynamically track `Average Realized Loss %`. If CLOB gapping pushes your average loss above 30%, the `min_WR` required for all tiers must dynamically increase.

**Trap B: The "Top-Rung Only" Skew (DupGuard Threat)**
You use a 2-rung ladder (min delta 0.04). Suppose you set limits at $0.82 and $0.88. In many cases, only the $0.88 rung will fill before the market resolves to $0.96. Your average entry is now $0.88, not $0.84. Your profit drops to 9%. If your loss remains 30%, **the BEWR on this specific trade jumps to 77%**.
* **If wrong:** Your metrics show a healthy 73% WR, but because you are heavily weighted in $0.88 single-fills, you are mathematically bleeding EV while thinking you have an edge.
* **Fix:** Track WR and EV separately for "Full Fill" vs "Partial Fill" trades. 

**Trap C: Liquidity Exhaustion at Tiers 3 & 4**
