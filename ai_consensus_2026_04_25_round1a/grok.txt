**1. Tier Table (Graduated bet_max ladder)**

Bet sizing rule (applied to all tiers): `position_bet = max($5, min(0.06 × current_bankroll, tier_bet_max))`. This keeps the existing 6% target but enforces the hard dollar cap per tier. Per-market exposure remains capped at 2× bet_max (DupGuard + 0.04 min delta). Only high-conviction entries (9+ edge points from 4-AI consensus + time-decay bonus) are allowed.

- **Tier 0 (Testing / Pre-Data)**: n_trades < 50, assumed_WR=0.65 (prior), bankroll_floor=$250 → **bet_max=$15**
- **Tier 1 (Initial Validation)**: n_trades ≥50, rolling_WR ≥0.71 (last 50 trades), bankroll_floor=$600 → **bet_max=$40**
- **Tier 2 (Proven)**: n_trades ≥150, rolling_WR ≥0.76 (last 100 trades), bankroll_floor=$1,800 → **bet_max=$95**
- **Tier 3 (Graduate)**: n_trades ≥350, rolling_WR ≥0.79 (last 150 trades), bankroll_floor=$5,000 → **bet_max=$225**
- **Tier 4 (Scaled/Elite)**: n_trades ≥750, rolling_WR ≥0.81 (last 200 trades) *and* lifetime_WR ≥0.77, bankroll_floor=$12,000 → **bet_max=$475** (or min($475, ~0.055 × current_bankroll) to allow limited further compounding at large sizes)

This directly mirrors the SHORT_TERM pattern (graduation spirit around ~100–150 trades with a high WR bar) but calibrates the WR thresholds downward because we expect true performance in the 72–80% range for this 14% gross edge strategy (not 95%+ like NEAR_CERTAIN). The n_trades thresholds are deliberately high because this specific 0.80-0.88 + early CLOB exit + stop-ladder mechanic has *zero* historical data. Bankroll floors ensure scaling is “earned” via both performance *and* capital growth. At $10k–15k bankroll you would need Tier 3–4 (proven track record) to escape the tiny $15–$95 range that currently stagnates growth.

**2. Drawdown circuit-breaker**

- Drop **1 tier** immediately if *any* of: peak-to-trough drawdown from ATH >20%, rolling_WR on last 30 trades <0.62, or bankroll falls below the current tier’s floor.
- Drop **to Tier 0** (or pause new entries for 48h) if DD >35%, rolling_WR on last 20 trades <0.55, *or* cumulative P/L since strategy start (or last tier unlock) < –15% of bankroll at the start of that period after ≥50 trades.
- Re-qualification: Must complete ≥25 new trades meeting (or exceeding) the target tier’s rolling_WR threshold before climbing back. Re-evaluate tier eligibility after every closed trade.

This is protective but not overly twitchy — normal variance in a ~75% WR strategy can produce short losing runs. It also adds the absolute P/L kill-switch you need for an untested zone.

**3. Kelly/variance reasoning — when higher caps become “safe”**

Assume realized economics close to your description: ~+14% on wins (0.84 avg entry → 0.96 exit), ~–23% to –27% on losses (stop-ladder at 0.94/0.92/0.90 + time decay on the remainder). This implies break-even WR ≈ 62–67% (exact number moves with realized avg loss; track this separately from raw WR).

Simplified Kelly approximation for asymmetric payoff: f* ≈ (p × net_odds_on_win – q) / net_odds_on_win. At a true 76% WR this yields full-Kelly fractions of ~25–35% of bankroll. Even at a more modest true 72–74% WR, full Kelly is still >12–20%. Our 6% bet_pct is therefore a conservative fractional Kelly (roughly 1/4 to 1/6 Kelly), leaving substantial margin for parameter uncertainty, fat tails, execution slippage, and correlation across simultaneous ladders.

**Higher bet_max tiers become statistically safe when the lower bound of the confidence interval on WR is comfortably above break-even (~68–70%) with acceptable ruin risk.** With binomial variance, n=50 at an observed 72% WR still has a wide 90% CI (roughly ±11–13%). By n=150–200 at observed 77–78% WR, the lower 90% CI tightens to ~71–73% — enough for positive expected growth even after slippage and with the stop-ladder. This is why the table requires n≥150 for $95+ bets and n≥350 for $225+. Below these thresholds, a streak of stops or a regime shift can turn a seemingly positive strategy negative in real dollars. Variance dominates early; the tier gates are deliberately data-heavy.

**4. Win rate assumption for Tier 1 (pre-data)**

**Yes, 65% is the right neutral prior.** It sits squarely in the middle of your stated 63–68% break-even range at 14% gross edge. It is slightly optimistic (reflecting the 4-AI Haiku-style filter + 9+ edge points + <4h time-decay bonus) without being reckless. We should not assume 72%+ until live data arrives. A Bayesian update (beta prior roughly equivalent to 20–30 pseudo-observations at 65%) is ideal once the first 30–50 trades are in. If early results come in at 58–62%, the strategy should be paused or re-examined even at Tier 0 sizing.

**5. Traps/gotchas in this design**

- **Liquidity and slippage (biggest risk at scale)**: Polymarket CLOB is often thin above $50–100 notional, especially near 0.96 or on the stop ladder (0.94→0.92→0.90). Realized edge will likely be 8–11% net on wins and –28% to –35% on stops vs. paper. Add a pre-entry liquidity/depth check; auto-reduce size 30–50% if opposing volume is insufficient. Track realized $/trade (not just WR) as a secondary graduation gate.
- **Small-sample variance / lucky streaks**: n=40–60 is still noisy. A hot streak could unlock Tier 1 prematurely. The rolling_WR windows (last 50/100/150) and volatility breaker (5 losses in ~7–10 trades or recent WR <0.62) mitigate this.
- **Correlation and concentration**: Even with DupGuard (max 2 per market, 0.04 delta), 8–12 concurrent positions in related events can create effective 15–30% bankroll exposure. Add a global active-exposure cap (e.g. 25–30% of bankroll across all open ladders).
- **Realized economics vs. theory**: If stops trigger more often or decay is worse than modeled, effective BE WR rises to ~70%+. Pure WR gating is insufficient — require cumulative net P/L > +0.8% of total capital risked as a co-condition for Tier 2+.
- **Model/AI decay or regime shift**: The 4-AI consensus performed well on NEAR_CERTAIN paper data but live execution broke earlier versions. The new zone has zero history. If net P/L is flat or negative after ~75–100 trades, freeze at Tier 0 and review filters. Edge_points should be logged and back-tested against outcomes.
- **Bankroll scaling at the high end**: Pure dollar bet_max creates stagnation above ~$20–30k. Tier 4’s soft % cap helps, but you may eventually want a sqrt(bankroll) multiplier post-Tier 3.
- **Operational**: Min $5 order is respected, but very small bankrolls (<$100) should probably pause. Proxy/geoblock issues from the prior live run must be solved before scaling. Early-exit CLOB limit orders at 0.96 introduce fill risk vs. “never wait for UMA.”

This design prioritizes survival and data collection in an untested zone while still allowing meaningful compounding once the edge is demonstrated. It is deliberately slower to scale than a proven strategy would be. Start live at Tier 0 only, log every edge_point breakdown, realized fill prices, and exact P/L, then tighten thresholds after the first 100 trades if the data justifies it. The combination of high n gates, rolling WR, bankroll floors, and DD breakers should prevent reckless growth while still rewarding sustained outperformance.
